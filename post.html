SEPA vs. SWIFT Transfers: Which One is Right for Your Business?

When sending international payments, choosing the right method can save your business time and money. Two common options are SEPA and SWIFT transfers but which one fits your needs?

What is SEPA?

SEPA (Single Euro Payments Area) is designed for fast and low-cost euro transfers within Europe. Covering 36 countries, SEPA payments are usually completed within a day and often come with little to no fees.

Best for: Euro payments inside Europe
Pros: Fast, low-cost, simpleLimitation: Euro only, Europe-only

What is SWIFT?

SWIFT is a global network connecting 11,000+ banks worldwide. It supports multiple currencies, making it ideal for international payments outside Europe. However, SWIFT transfers may take several days and involve higher fees due to intermediary banks. Best for: Global payments in any currency
Pros: Worldwide reach, multi-currency
Limitation: Slower, higher fees

Which Should You Choose?

Use SEPA for euro transactions within Europe — fast and affordable.
Use SWIFT for global payments in multiple currencies — wider coverage, but higher costs.
